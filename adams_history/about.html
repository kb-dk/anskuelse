About the KB Labs presentation of Adams Illustrated Panorama of History, 1878

Background

This piece was dititized by Aarhus University Library, Emdrup, Denmark and is under CC-BY-SA license. More data about the piece are available at galleri.au.dk. The original is 70 x 673 cm and digitized in 62 DPI, to fit within the technical limitations of the TIFF format. It can be downloaded as a single file:

  • 104345x11288 pixels (1177 MPixel), TIFF, 3.3GB (raw digitization used for this page)
  • 65500x7086 pixels (464 MPixel), JPEG, 96MB (max dimensions allowed by the JPEG format)
  • 32000x3462 pixels (110 MPixel), JPEG, 29MB (max compatibility with JPEG viewers)

Technical

  • The base presentation was created using the excellent tool libvips for image processing and the equally excellent OpenSeadragon for display
  • Bookmarking functionality was added using callback-hooks and the zoom & pan-API from OpenSeadragon, with a bit of JavaScript-glue
  • The Area Of Interest proof of concept (hover over the globe in the lower left corner) was added by hand, using the overlay-API from OpenSeadragon

Plans

We see the current KB Labs-product as a quick take and would like to extend it.

  • One of the interesting properties of the Adams piece is the huge amount of distinct information that it holds. There are thousands of areas of interests, from a 10 horned beast to the cardiovascular system. This calls for an accessible annotation system with a visualization of the areas of interest that does not overwhelm the user. One technical challenge here is that OpenSeadragon overlays does not scale well into thousands of overlays
  • The Adams piece is visually impressive and displays well on large screens. Adding a demo-mode where the view is automatically panned & zoomed is one way of keep such a display interesting. It could be a random walk or it could be between pre-selected areas of interest. It would combine well with annotations
